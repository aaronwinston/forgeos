# Market Research Playbook

## Core Principle

Research first. Marketing second.

Strong research creates authority because it discovers useful truth before it tries to package that truth.

## Why Research Matters

Research can:

- establish category authority
- create durable storylines
- support sales and marketing
- inform analyst and press conversations
- generate original insights

## Research System

### 1. Define the Objective

Clarify:

- What question are we answering?
- Who needs this insight?
- What decision or narrative will it support?
- What would make the research credible?

### 2. Build the Working Group

Include:

- executive sponsor
- research lead
- content strategy lead
- market insights lead
- communications lead
- product or subject matter expert
- design lead
- activation owner

### 3. Design the Research

Decide whether the work should be:

- externally validated research
- internal telemetry analysis
- qualitative interviews
- survey-based analysis
- mixed-method research

### 4. Analyze Before Writing

Hold structured analysis sessions before drafting the report.

Ask:

- What surprised us?
- What changed from prior assumptions?
- What does this mean for the audience?
- What is the credible narrative?
- What proof supports it?

### 5. Build a Data-to-Story Map

Map each finding to:

- audience insight
- narrative implication
- proof artifact
- potential asset

### 6. Activate the Findings

Research should feed:

- reports
- blog posts
- PR
- analyst relations
- sales enablement
- executive narratives
- social content
- webinars
- lifecycle campaigns

## Agent Instructions

When using research:

- Do not chase headlines before the insight exists.
- Do not overstate findings.
- Always connect data to a narrative and an activation path.
