# Analyst Relations Playbook

## Core Principle

Analyst relations is a narrative reinforcement system.

Analysts do not remember one interaction. They remember patterns.

## The System

### Narrative Layer

Define a clear, defensible point of view.

### Engagement Layer

Use the right engagement format:

- Briefings — communicate updates and vision
- Inquiries — gather feedback and influence thinking
- Advisory sessions — explore strategic direction
- Document reviews — test messaging and positioning

### Influence Layer

Influence compounds through:

- Frequency
- Consistency
- Depth
- Follow-through

## Briefing Principles

- Start with the key points.
- Reinforce them repeatedly.
- Focus on market meaning and customer impact.
- Keep demos short.
- Do not over-explain the market.
- Do not rely on hype metrics.

## Inquiry Principles

Use inquiries for:

- message testing
- market validation
- roadmap feedback
- competitive positioning
- analyst worldview mapping

## Editorial Integration

Strong content should reinforce the same narrative analysts hear.

Content → briefing → inquiry → research → content

## Agent Instructions

Use this file when creating analyst-facing content, briefing outlines, inquiry questions, or influence strategies. Keep it general and avoid sensitive account-specific details.
